Hello! I am a Computer Science PhD student at the University of Pennsylvania (2023–) advised by Professor Jianbo Shi, and a visiting PhD student at Berkeley AI Research (Jan 2026–).

My research takes a bottom-up view of generative and perception models — looking at the data they consume, the representations they form, and the traces they leave in their outputs — to better understand and control how these models perceive, generate, and reason.

  • Representation: the concepts these models encode, and how to discover, ground, and steer them.
  • Explainability: tracing generative outputs back to the initial noise or source model.

I am supported by the NDSEG Fellowship, and have interned at Meta Superintelligence Labs (2025), Adobe Research (2024), and Meta FAIR (2022). I received my bachelor's and master's degrees in Computer Science (Course 6-3) from MIT, where I conducted research at CSAIL and the Quest for Intelligence.

If you'd like to chat about research or collaborate, please reach out!
